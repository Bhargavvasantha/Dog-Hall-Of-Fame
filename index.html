Hero Dogs

Hero Dogs places service dogs with veterans of the U.S. military and first-responders with disabilities. Disabilities do not need to be service connected.It’s impossible to fully thank a veteran or first-responder, but a service dog can go a long way in showing our appreciation. A well-trained, eager-to-please dog can do everything from retrieving items, opening doors and turning on lights, to alerting their partners to sounds and waking them from nightmares. In short, a service dog can be invaluable

Working Dogs

Anyone who is passionate about helping dogs live long, happy and healthy lives would say that all dogs should have some sort of job. Giving a dog a job they enjoy can help to prevent an almost endless list of potential behavior issues (such as excessive barking, inappropriate chewing and digging, separation problems, and more). It can also help them maintain overall physical wellness. So, by this definition, all dogs are, or at least should be, working dogs.

Companion Dogs

A companion dog can provide a veteran with PTSD critical benefits: a loyal companion when depression and anxiety take over, a trusted companion for fun and stress reduction, a way to express love and compassion, and a good reason to spend time outdoors.
